second to last day in January, will start a new february devlog in a couple of days.
Lots of non-proto tower creating happening this week. tons of focus on some complex pieces.
Discovered a bug (in my opinion) while minting "BugTower". That was interesting.
The bug is that the Stargaze frontend converts gifs->MP4s because MP4s are more efficient.
But the program used to convert (ffmpeg) does something to the final frame that truncates it's length.
So basically, if you have a gif that's 10 frames of 1 second each, the mp4 on stargaze will look like this:
- 9 frames that are 1 second
- 10th frame is 100 milliseconds (or something really short like that)
- the workaround is to make a piece that's 11 frames, where both frame 10 and 11 are the same, effectively fixing the issue.
- this seems like an unnecessary workaround if the final frame wasn't truncated.
